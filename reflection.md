# Profile Comparison Reflections

## Chill Lofi vs Focused Lofi

Both want lofi at similar energy (0.38 vs 0.42) but different moods. The top results overlap heavily because the energy gap is only 0.04 — too small to meaningfully separate them. The difference appears at #4–5 where mood proximity tips the scale. A focused listener needs slightly more mental alertness than a chill one, and the algorithm reflects that at the margins.

---

## Focused Lofi vs Relaxed Lofi

Energy 0.42 vs 0.34 — a bigger gap. Coffee Shop Stories (jazz, energy 0.37) jumps to #2 in the Relaxed list because 0.37 is closer to 0.34 than to 0.42. Deep Blue (energy 0.33) also rises. The Relaxed profile correctly gravitates toward slightly quieter, lower-energy songs within the same genre family.

---

## Pop Happy vs High-Energy Pop

Same genre, energy shifts from 0.80 to 0.90. The #1 result flips — Sunrise City leads Pop Happy but drops to #4 in High-Energy Pop because Gym Hero (energy 0.93) is a tighter energy match. A 0.10 shift in energy preference was enough to swap the top song entirely.

---

## Deep Intense Rock vs Sad Acoustic Folk

Most opposite pairing. Rock profile surfaces Storm Runner, Pulse Wave, Circuit Breaker (energy 0.88–0.97, all electric). Folk profile surfaces Hollow Roads, Rainy Window, Spacewalk Thoughts (energy 0.22–0.31, all acoustic). Zero overlap. Two users at opposite ends of energy and acousticness land in completely different corners of the catalog — the clearest proof the scoring is working.

---

## Chill Lofi vs High-Energy Pop

Most extreme comparison. No songs appear in both lists at all. Chill Lofi pulls from the acoustic/ambient cluster, High-Energy Pop pulls from the electronic/pop cluster. Energy at 50% weight is the dominant separator — when two profiles are this far apart in energy preference, genre and mood become secondary.

---

## Pop Happy vs Sad Acoustic Folk

Opposite moods and opposite energy. Sunrise City (happy, 0.82 energy) leads Pop Happy. Hollow Roads (sad, 0.31 energy) scores a perfect 1.00 for Sad Acoustic Folk. No overlap. When mood and energy both point in opposite directions simultaneously, the two profiles land in completely separate parts of the catalog with no accidental crossover.
